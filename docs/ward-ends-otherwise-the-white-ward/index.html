Ward Ends, Otherwise the White Ward

@I@
Forty weeks, then forty-two. Then the days
played peekaboo. By Wednesday noon,
hours kicked, and at forty-three, minutes
scratched. We got a pamphlet; doctor
withdrew us four as if to reassure.

Induced, the labour is said to be.
Post-term, the baby is said to be.
Late, the mother is said to be.

Westminster Bridge and its amber
lights hazing my tears. For quick
respite, wind freezes them in place.
As though I need to exhibit such freedoms.

The consultant said we could be in the room.
Why wasn't he?
@II@
About our baby, paralysed, I would kill
to stop the whine. I could kill,
dare I to move, seven thousand hours
of life accrued. Screams pierce ordinarily.
This one's intentless, an incessant,
titanic broadcast of suffering?
Inaccessible, the scream-inducers lure me
to paralysis against the pleaing shriek.
Cease the pain and overturn the
futile cries. There wasn't a champagne
tap but a fast bleep.

@III@
If only I knew how to save a life,
castaway on this north wing, seventh floor.
Receptionists and signs donate a buzz of fame
opposite. Gynae: where a whole new world of
'lap and dye,' or 'D and C' awaits diploid
destiny.
Obs and gynae, obs and gynae, obs
and gynae.
Obs and gynae, sobs and gynae, sobs
and gynae.
Thursday 24 March 2022
Jacob Fisher
