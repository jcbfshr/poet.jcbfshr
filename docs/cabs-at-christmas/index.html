Cabs at Christmas

The taxi driver desolately peers
With beautiful blues blaring in his cab.
His cheeks becoming stained and daubed with tears.
Awaiting blithely that next drunken drab.

His sharpened scalpel, recently stolen,
Now covets blood once more on Christmas eve.
'Crime pays the dime on time,' he chants his omen.
Snowy teardrops always join begs of leave.

This sweet piano dampens heavy souls
Because the midnight sun will never set.
'Baby, baby, don't you know these rolling roads
Make the midnight monster in me?' Forget...

Mind of murder, scalpel of salvation;
Now I bid, rid of this cold creation.
Monday 24 May 2021
Jacob Fisher
